An honest chart wearing a costume. The planet's position is the ensemble mean of whichever datasets are checked below the controls — all six by default (NASA GISTEMP, HadCRUT5, NOAAGlobalTemp, Berkeley Earth, ERA5, JMA); uncheck down to one to fly a single instrument's record, or add them back one by one and watch the path barely move (common 1951–80 baseline, Gaussian-smoothed σ ≈ 4 yr): horizontal — temperature anomaly; vertical — warming rate. The halo around the planet is the live disagreement between the six datasets. The "sun" marks the mean 1880–1969 state and its faint rings the orbit-era envelope; the planet's color runs blue → ember with the anomaly itself. Everything else — stars, corona, comet tail — is staging, and changes no data point. Toggle the axes off for a clean cinematic view (good for screen recording). The score is an original ambient piece synthesized live in your browser — organ ostinato, drone, and a clock tick in the spirit of a certain film about leaving home — with the mix swelling as the anomaly grows; no recorded music is loaded (the actual Interstellar soundtrack is copyrighted, and overlaying it on a public post invites a mute or takedown).
