The Braid — six climate datasets escaping orbit together

the braid · ensemble state-space · six datasets, one trajectory

Six cameras, filming one escape.

Every climate dataset is a different camera pointed at the same planet. Run all six through state space — position vs. speed of warming — and they braid into a single rope. The rope's thickness is the measurement disagreement; its path is the climate. Press play and watch six independent measurements orbit together for a century, then leave together. Nobody's instrument is wrong. The orbit is just over.

Same data as the six-dataset comparison: NASA GISTEMP v4, HadCRUT5, NOAAGlobalTemp v6, Berkeley Earth, ERA5 and JMA, each rebaselined to a common 1951–80 zero by a constant offset. Each series is smoothed with a Gaussian kernel (σ ≈ 4 yr) inside its own span; the rate is the centered derivative of the smoothed series. The translucent tube is drawn through the per-year envelope of all datasets covering that year — its width is the real, honest disagreement between independent measurement systems. The strands do not all start together — each begins when its instrument record does (open ring, year noted): —.
