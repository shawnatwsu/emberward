Six thermometers, one answer.

Four independent observational groups (NASA, the UK Met Office, NOAA, Berkeley Earth), the Japan Meteorological Agency, and Europe's ERA5 reanalysis all measure Earth's temperature with different stations, different ocean data, and different methods. Put on a common baseline and overlaid, they are nearly a single line — the disagreement between them is a rounding error next to the warming itself.

Global mean surface temperature, annual — all six series anomaly (°C) vs the common 1951–80 baseline

Each series keeps its native methodology and is shifted only by a constant — its own 1951–80 mean — so the curves share a zero without any reshaping. Observational datasets (GISTEMP, HadCRUT5, NOAAGlobalTemp, Berkeley Earth, JMA) are annual means of station and ship/buoy measurements; ERA5 is a physics-based reanalysis and its final point is a year-to-date mean, drawn as an open ring.
